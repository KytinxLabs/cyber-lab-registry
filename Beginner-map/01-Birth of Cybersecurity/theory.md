# 📖 Chapter 0 — The Birth of Cybersecurity

> _"Before you learn how to protect systems, you must first understand how they broke."_

---

## 🌐 Before Cybersecurity Had a Name

In the early days of the Internet, there were:

- ❌ No firewalls
- ❌ No antivirus software
- ❌ No intrusion detection systems

Computers trusted each other by default.

The Internet was small, academic, and built on curiosity—not caution.

No one thought about defending machines because no one imagined they would need to.

---

## 🗓️ November 1988 — A Mistake That Changed Everything

In November 1988, graduate student **Robert Tappan Morris** released a small experimental program onto the Internet.

It wasn't written to destroy.

It wasn't written to steal.

It was written to measure the size of the Internet.

But the program had a flaw.

It could copy itself.

And it didn't know when to stop.

---

## 🦠 The Morris Worm

The program began spreading from machine to machine.

- Replicating silently
- Re-entering systems it had already infected
- Copying itself again...
- And again...
- And again...

Within hours:

- Computers slowed to a crawl
- Systems became unusable
- Administrators unplugged machines from the network
- Nearly **10% of the entire Internet** was affected

For the first time in history, the Internet experienced a large-scale cyber incident—not because of malicious intent, but because of **unchecked self-replicating code**.

---

## ⚠️ The Real Damage Wasn't the Worm

The Morris Worm didn't:

- Delete files
- Steal data
- Spy on users

Yet it caused widespread disruption.

### Why?

Because there were:

- No safeguards
- No rate limits
- No kill switches
- No incident response plans

> _(You'll learn about all of these in upcoming chapters.)_

The world learned an important lesson:

> **Even curiosity can become dangerous when systems lack defensive design.**

---

## 🛡️ And That's When Cybersecurity Was Born

The Morris Worm changed computing forever.

Its impact led to:

- The creation of the first **Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT)**
- Recognition that systems must be designed with security in mind
- The emergence of ethical hacking—understanding how systems fail in order to protect them

Cybersecurity wasn't invented in a laboratory.

## **It was forced into existence by a mistake.**

## 📝 Historical Note

> **The first malware was _Creeper_ (1971), not the Morris Worm.**

Creeper was an experimental self-replicating program that caused little harm.

The Morris Worm became historically significant because it triggered the first major Internet-wide security incident and fundamentally changed how the world approached computer security.
